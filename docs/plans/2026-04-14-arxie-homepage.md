# Arxie

*Research moves faster when your papers are searchable, structured, and grounded.*

Arxie is an AI research workspace built for serious literature work. It does not just answer questions about papers. It builds a living paperbase behind every answer so you can search a corpus, inspect figures, compare methods, trace evidence, and turn that work into reviews, proposals, and research memos.

## What You Can Do

Ask Arxie a research question:

> What are the most credible recent approaches to long-context reasoning?

Arxie does more than retrieve a few abstracts. It can:

- search a persistent paper corpus by topic, date, venue, author, method, dataset, metric, and tag
- read full papers and preserve section-level structure
- extract methods, datasets, metrics, glossary terms, findings, engineering tricks, figures, and tables
- compare papers across benchmarks and experimental settings
- show the evidence behind every important claim
- generate literature reviews, proposal drafts, and evidence-backed summaries

## Why It Feels Different

Most research assistants are session-based. They search live APIs, summarize a few papers, and forget everything when the conversation ends.

Arxie keeps a durable paperbase:

- a canonical database of papers and metadata
- full-text chunks and semantic search
- extracted research structure
- figure and table references
- evidence spans tied back to source text
- saved workspaces and proposal flows

That means you can come back tomorrow and keep working on the same research landscape instead of starting over.

## A Typical Workflow

### 1. Search a field

Start broad:

> Find recent work on retrieval-augmented reasoning after 2024.

Then narrow it:

- only benchmark papers
- only papers using synthetic data
- only papers evaluated on HotpotQA or GSM8K
- only papers with open-access PDFs

### 2. Open a paper deeply

For each paper, Arxie can show:

- title, authors, venue, and date
- abstract and section tree
- parsed full text
- figures and captions
- extracted method
- datasets used
- metrics reported
- benchmark results
- key findings and limitations

If Arxie says a paper improved a metric, you can inspect the exact evidence instead of trusting a freeform summary.

### 3. Compare papers as data

Ask:

- Compare these 15 papers by dataset and metric.
- Which methods report the strongest gains on long-context tasks?
- What engineering tricks appear across efficient fine-tuning papers?
- Show me the result tables and ablation figures for the top 5.

Arxie turns papers into a structured comparison surface.

### 4. Build a research deliverable

Once the evidence is assembled, Arxie can produce:

- structured literature reviews
- proposal drafts
- evidence maps
- benchmark comparison tables
- research gap summaries
- branch-based hypothesis comparisons

You move from paper search to decision-ready output in one workspace.

## Trust and Verification

Arxie is built for defensibility, not just fluency.

Important outputs are grounded by:

- inline citations
- source-linked evidence spans
- extracted result rows
- figure and table provenance
- explicit uncertainty when evidence is mixed

The goal is simple: make it easy to trust what you read and easy to inspect what matters.

## Two Layers, One Product

Arxie has two parts:

- **Arxie Assistant**: the interface for search, chat, lit review, proposal drafting, and synthesis
- **Paperbase Platform**: the backend corpus system that stores papers, parses PDFs, extracts structure, indexes search, and powers comparison workflows

As a user, you should experience one product. Under the hood, the assistant and the paperbase have different jobs.

## Who It Is For

Arxie is for people who need more than paper search:

- researchers exploring a technical field
- startup teams doing market and technical diligence
- analysts building evidence-backed reports
- research engineers comparing methods and benchmark results
- agentic workflows that need safe, structured access to papers

## Core Promise

Arxie gives you a research operating system:

- ask questions naturally
- inspect real evidence
- compare papers structurally
- extract reusable knowledge
- keep your work over time

It is not just a chat interface over papers. It is a persistent, grounded workspace for research decisions.
